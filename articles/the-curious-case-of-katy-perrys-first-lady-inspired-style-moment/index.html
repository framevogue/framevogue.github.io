The Curious Case of Katy Perry's First Lady-Inspired Style Moment

March 10, 2026 - 4 min read

There's a new chapter in the evolving Katy Perry and Justin Trudeau story. After quietly confirming their relationship on Instagram late last year, the pair took a significant public step this week by appearing together at the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland — their first high-profile joint appearance on the global stage.

A Restrained Debut on the World Stage

On Monday, January 19, Perry and the former Canadian prime minister were spotted together in Davos, and the pop star's fashion choices immediately caught attention — not for their usual maximalism, but for their surprising restraint.

For the occasion, Perry leaned into a polished, head-to-toe Jacquemus ensemble that felt deliberately understated. She wore a cream cropped cardigan in a soft merino wool blend, featuring a subtle scoop neckline and sculptural curved sleeves that nodded to sixties elegance. The look was completed with a coordinating knit pencil skirt, creating a silhouette that felt refined and intentional without sacrificing personality.

Her accessories were understated but deliberate: square drop earrings, angular tortoiseshell Jacquemus and Linda Farrow Spiaggia sunglasses, and her hair pulled back into a sleek, middle-parted chignon. The overall effect was quiet, elegant, and refreshingly pared back — a noticeable departure from the bold theatrics Perry typically favors.

Day Two: Sharp Tailoring With an Edge

The following day, Perry returned with a sharper, more structured approach that still felt unmistakably her own. She stepped out in a navy pinstripe Mugler Re Edit Les Salades wool jacket featuring a cinched waist and an integrated white tank detail. Paired with matching straight-leg trousers, the look struck a confident balance between power dressing and modern ease.

In another moment captured during the week, Perry was seen carrying Jacquemus's caramel-toned Valèrie bag, folded neatly under her arm, and layering up in a Bottega Veneta shearling coat from the Resort 2023 collection — both stylish and practical for the chilly Swiss climate.

What the Styling Choices Say

Perry's Davos wardrobe reads as a considered recalibration. The brands she chose — Jacquemus, Mugler, Bottega Veneta — are all associated with a certain kind of elevated, European-inflected cool: fashion-forward without being ostentatious, polished without being stiff.

The "first lady-inspired" quality of the looks isn't accidental. Appearing alongside a former head of government on a global stage calls for a different visual register than a stadium tour or a Met Gala. Perry seems to understand that, and her styling reflects it.

Practical Tips

  • When dressing for formal international events, opt for clean silhouettes and muted palettes that photograph well and read as composed rather than attention-seeking.
  • Coordinating knitwear sets — cardigan and skirt or trousers — offer an effortlessly polished look without requiring complex styling.
  • A structured jacket with a cinched waist is one of the most versatile pieces in a travel wardrobe: it elevates casual basics and holds its own in formal settings.
  • Investing in a quality shearling coat pays dividends in cold climates — it's both functional and inherently luxurious-looking.

Conclusion

Katy Perry's Davos wardrobe was a masterclass in contextual dressing — choosing clothes that suit the moment rather than the brand. Whether intentional or instinctive, her shift toward refined, European-influenced tailoring for this particular appearance felt appropriate, considered, and quietly impressive. Sometimes the most interesting fashion story isn't the loudest one.

FAQ

Q: What did Katy Perry wear at the World Economic Forum in Davos? Perry wore a cream Jacquemus merino wool cardigan and coordinating knit pencil skirt on day one, followed by a navy pinstripe Mugler wool jacket with matching trousers on day two. She also carried a Jacquemus Valèrie bag and wore a Bottega Veneta shearling coat.

Q: Why is Katy Perry's Davos style being described as "first lady-inspired"? Her looks were notably restrained and polished compared to her usual bold aesthetic — featuring clean silhouettes, muted palettes, and understated accessories that evoked the composed elegance often associated with political spouses at formal international events.

Q: Who is Katy Perry currently in a relationship with? According to reports, Perry confirmed a relationship with former Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau on Instagram late last year, with their Davos appearance marking their first major public outing together.

Q: What is Jacquemus known for in fashion? Jacquemus is a French fashion house founded by Simon Porte Jacquemus, known for its minimalist yet playful aesthetic, sculptural silhouettes, and sun-drenched Mediterranean sensibility. It has become a favorite among celebrities and fashion editors alike.

Q: How do you dress appropriately for a high-profile international event? Focus on clean, well-tailored silhouettes in neutral or muted tones. Choose quality fabrics that photograph well and hold their shape. Keep accessories deliberate rather than excessive, and prioritize comfort alongside polish — you'll likely be on your feet and in front of cameras for extended periods.
