Licorice Extract Might Be the Quiet Hero Your Skincare Routine Is Missing

March 2, 2026 - 4 min read

Licorice probably calls to mind old-fashioned candy, herbal tea, or that distinctly sweet flavor your grandmother swore could soothe an upset stomach. But in skincare circles, licorice extract has built an entirely different reputation — one grounded in science, subtlety, and genuinely visible results.

Dermatologists now widely recommend it for achieving a more even skin tone, fading discoloration, and gently boosting radiance — all without the harsh side effects that accompany some of its louder, better-known counterparts.

What Makes Licorice Extract Effective?

The magic lies in naturally occurring compounds within licorice root — particularly glabridin and liquiritin. These compounds help inhibit the production of excess melanin, which is responsible for dark spots, uneven pigmentation, and stubborn post-acne marks.

Unlike harsher brightening agents that can leave skin feeling stripped, raw, or reactive, licorice works gently. Rather than bleaching or forcing the skin into submission, it guides it back toward balance. That's why people with sensitive, reactive, or redness-prone skin gravitate toward it.

Brightening Without the Burn

Licorice extract is one of the rare ingredients that brightens without irritating. While Vitamin C and retinoids can absolutely deliver transformative results, they often come with an adjustment period — a "tingle" or flush that isn't always reassuring.

Licorice, by contrast, tends to feel soothing from the very first application. It helps reduce inflammation, calms stressed skin, and evens out discoloration gradually. Think of it as a slow, steady improvement rather than an overnight transformation.

A Natural Ally Against Hyperpigmentation

Whether you're dealing with sun spots, melasma, uneven pigmentation, or marks left behind by breakouts, licorice extract can help fade those darker patches toward your natural skin tone. When incorporated into serums, eye creams, or moisturizers and used consistently, it can deliver noticeable improvements in clarity — not overnight, but reliably over time.

Who Should Use Licorice Extract?

Licorice extract is surprisingly versatile. It works well for:

  • Uneven skin tone and dullness
  • Post-acne marks and mild hyperpigmentation
  • Redness and inflammation
  • Sensitive or compromised skin barriers

It also plays well with other actives. Paired with niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, or gentle exfoliants, it helps optimize everything else in your routine without competing or causing irritation.

Practical Tips

  • Apply licorice extract serums in the morning and evening for consistent, cumulative brightening over time.
  • Layer it under SPF during the day — it won't make you sun-sensitive, but protecting your skin from UV damage is essential for any pigmentation-fighting routine.
  • Pair licorice extract with Vitamin C for a synergistic brightening boost, but introduce one at a time to see how your skin responds.
  • If you have sensitive skin, licorice extract is an excellent alternative to stronger brighteners like hydroquinone or high-percentage acids.
  • Be patient — licorice works gradually, so expect to see meaningful changes after six to eight weeks of consistent use.

Conclusion

Licorice extract isn't flashy. It doesn't promise overnight miracles or deliver dramatic before-and-after photos. What it offers instead is something more sustainable: clarity without chaos, brightness without burnout, and balance instead of irritation. In a skincare industry crowded with aggressive actives and bold claims, licorice extract stands out as a quiet reminder that the best ingredients often work gently — and exceptionally well.

FAQ

Q: What skin concerns does licorice extract address? Licorice extract is most effective for hyperpigmentation, dark spots, uneven skin tone, post-acne marks, redness, and general dullness. It also has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe sensitive or reactive skin.

Q: Can licorice extract be used with other active ingredients? Yes. It pairs well with niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, and gentle exfoliants. For best results with Vitamin C, use them at different times of day or alternate days to avoid potential interactions.

Q: How long does it take to see results from licorice extract? Most users notice subtle improvements in skin tone and clarity after six to eight weeks of consistent daily use. Results are gradual and cumulative rather than immediate.

Q: Is licorice extract safe for sensitive skin? Yes — it's one of the most gentle brightening ingredients available and is often recommended specifically for sensitive, reactive, or redness-prone skin types.

Q: Does licorice extract make skin more sun-sensitive? No, licorice extract does not increase photosensitivity. However, daily SPF is still essential when treating any form of hyperpigmentation, as UV exposure will counteract your progress.
