Every Red Carpet Look From the 2026 Critics Choice Awards

May 19, 2026 - 3 min read

The 2026 Critics Choice Awards lit up Santa Monica as Hollywood officially stepped into awards season mode. Held at the iconic Barker Hangar in California, the event marked the beginning of several months packed with major ceremonies, celebrity appearances, and serious Oscar buzz.

As the road to the Golden Globes and the Academy Awards begins, A-list stars are already making strategic appearances while celebrating the year's most talked-about film and television performances. The 31st annual Critics Choice Awards were once again hosted by Chelsea Handler, who returned for her fourth time, bringing humor and genuine familiarity to the evening.

A Star-Studded Nominee List

This year's nominee roster featured some of the biggest names in entertainment: Timothée Chalamet for Marty Supreme, Jessie Buckley for Hamnet, Emma Stone for Bugonia, and Ariana Grande for Wicked For Good. With such a high-profile guest list, the red carpet was always going to deliver.

Fashion That Took Risks

Known for being more relaxed than the Golden Globes or the Oscars, the Critics Choice Awards often give stars permission to experiment — to take bolder fashion risks without the weight of more formal ceremonies. This year was no exception.

The red carpet showcased an appealing tension between classic Hollywood glamour and genuinely modern design. Sleek silhouettes appeared alongside dramatic couture moments. Playful elegance held its own against more structured, architectural choices. In previous years, Nicole Kidman turned heads in tailored Saint Laurent suits, while Michelle Yeoh made headlines in a statement Balenciaga gown. The 2026 red carpet continued that tradition of memorable, individualistic dressing.

The Critics Choice Awards as a Fashion Preview

Because the Critics Choice Awards arrive early in the season, they often function as a sartorial preview — a glimpse at which stars, stylists, and aesthetics are setting the tone for the months ahead. This year suggested a move toward personal expression over rigid formality, with stars dressing in ways that felt authentic to them rather than conforming to a single red-carpet formula.

Conclusion

The 2026 Critics Choice Awards delivered exactly what a season-opening ceremony should: a blend of celebration, experimentation, and genuine style. As the awards season stretches ahead, the looks from this night offer an early, promising glimpse of what's to come — and a reminder that the best red-carpet fashion is always the kind that feels personal, not prescribed.
